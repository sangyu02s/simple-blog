There was once a boy who lived at the edge of a small village. Every evening, when the roads grew dark, he carried a tiny lamp in his hand. The lamp was not bright enough to light the whole road, but it was enough for the next few steps.

One night, he met an old woman who was afraid to walk home alone. Without hesitation, the boy walked beside her until she reached her door. On another evening, he shared his lamp with a child who had lost his way near the field.

People in the village began to say that the lamp itself was nothing special. What mattered was the heart of the one who carried it. The boy never argued. He simply smiled, lifted the little lamp higher, and kept walking into the dark with kindness.

In time, others started to bring their own lights. The road did not become bright all at once, but it became less lonely. And sometimes, that is how goodness begins: with one small light carried faithfully by one small hand.

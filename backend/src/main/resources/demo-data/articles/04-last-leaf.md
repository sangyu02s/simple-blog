At the end of summer, a girl noticed one leaf still hanging on a branch outside her window. Day after day, wind moved through the garden, and more leaves fell to the ground. Yet this one remained.

Her grandfather told her, "Sometimes the world keeps one small thing in place so that we remember not everything is meant to leave at once." The girl watched the leaf each morning before school and each evening before sleep.

When autumn came fully, the leaf finally drifted down in a soft golden circle. Instead of feeling sad, the girl smiled. She had learned that letting go is easier when something has stayed with us long enough to be loved.

Years later, she would still remember that leaf whenever life seemed to change too quickly. It reminded her that patience has its own quiet language.
